# Literature Survey Topic — Recsys Industry Trend & Future Planning (3 surveys)

**Created:** 2026-04-19

## Why surveyed
Three recent surveys (retrieval / sequential / generative recommendation) landed close together. As an ML engineer working in recsys, I want to triangulate them to understand where the industry actually is in 2024–2025, where it's heading, and where to invest engineering / learning effort next.

## Audience
Just me (ML engineer in recsys). Not a team-facing doc, not a publication.

## Project Context
I work on production recsys (classical discriminative pipelines: retrieval → pre-ranking → ranking → re-ranking). Generative recsys is exploration territory for me — not yet a production concern but I need to know whether/when it will be.

The survey output should answer:
1. Which classical retrieval/sequential techniques are still production-state-of-the-art vs. legacy.
2. What's the realistic timeline & maturity of generative recsys for production.
3. Which skills / research directions matter for the next 1–3 years of my work.
4. Where do the three surveys agree, disagree, or leave gaps.

A finding is "useful" if it informs a concrete decision: adopt a technique, deprecate one, study a topic, or wait it out.
